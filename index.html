// how i got here

My path into networking started during my Electrical Engineering degree, where I was first exposed to the CLI, routing and switching concepts, and the TCP/IP and OSI models. Something clicked — the logical, structured way networks communicate felt like a natural extension of the systems thinking I'd developed through engineering.

Since then I've been deliberately building my skills from the ground up. Rather than just reading about concepts, I learn by building labs, breaking things, and troubleshooting my way back. Every project on this site reflects that — working through real configurations, not just theory.

I pick up new technology quickly. Whether it's a new protocol, a vendor platform, or a tool I haven't used before — I figure it out, document it, and move forward. That's the kind of engineer I'm becoming.
